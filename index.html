LIFE EXPECTANCY

Life expectancy is a statistical measure of the average number
of years a person can expect to live.

Life expectancy is influenced by a number of factors including
access to healthcare, education, genetics, nutrition, sanitation,
choice of lifestyle and et cetera.

The chosen dataset for this visualisation was collected from the
Global Health Observatory (GHO) data respository under the World
Health Organization (WHO).

The dataset collected contains data from year 2000 to 2015 for
193 countries around the world.

AVERAGE LIFE EXPECTANCY FOR SIX CONTINENETS ACROSS THE WORLD FROM 2000 TO 2015

By looking at the graph below, we can tell that the continent South America has the highest value of average life expectancy - 77.65 while the continent Africa has the lowest value of average life expectancy - 58.79 among all other continents over the 16 years.

The difference of the average life expectancy value between South America and Africa is 18.86 which is a significant gap.

COUNTRIES WITH TOP 5 HIGHEST OR LOWEST VALUE OF AVERAGE LIFE EXPECTANCY ACROSS THE WORLD FROM 2000 TO 2015

From the graph, we can see that the top 3 countries with the highest average life expectancy value are Japan - 82.54, followed by Sweden - 82.52 and Iceland - 82.44.

From the graph, we can see that the top 3 countries with the lowest average life expectancy value are Sierra Leone - 46.11, followed by Central Africa Republic - 48.51 and Lesotho - 48.78.

RANKING AMONG THE TOP 10 COUNTRIES WITH THE HIGHEST AVERAGE LIFE EXPECTANCY OVER YEARS

We can observe that Japan has consistently ranked number 1 in terms of average life expectancy for many years, especially the year 2012, 2013, 2014 and 2015 which make it hit a four in a row straight for ranked number 1.

Next, we can see that Norway have seen significant improvements in life expectancy over the year 2002 to 2009 as it ranked number 10 in year 2002 and ranked number 1 in year 2009.

ADULT MORTALITY RATE IN DIFFERENT COUNTRIES ACROSS THE WORLD FROM 2000 TO 2015

We can see that the adult mortality rates have decreased in most countries over the time.

In the year 2000, Greenland has the lowest adult mortality rate - 12 and Zimbabwe has the highest adult mortality rate - 665.

In the year 2015, United States of Amerca has the lowest adult mortality rate - 13 and Lesotho has the highest adult mortality rate - 484.
